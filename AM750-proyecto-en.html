About the project

Am750 is a radio streaming which belongs to Grupo Octubre. It has been 10 years on air and is the house of a lot of famous hosts in Argentina. Between 2015-2019 it had a sustained growth in audience and seeks to position itself as one of the most impotant radios. The digital product team is asked to carry out a redesign that allows to increase the digital audience

My role

UX/UI Designer

Duration

4 meses

Product

Radio digital Am750

Problems

  • There is no information about the users, their behaviors and pain points.

Roadmap

Having a short period of time, we decided to adjust the roadmap considering it a starting point. We define a qualitative methodology, in order to identify the main needs of the users and understand the reason for the behaviors. We only analyze metrics at the beginning of the project to establish hypotheses and goals.

2.Define objectives

3. Hypothesis

4.Script and interviews

5.Interviews analysis

Research questions

The research questions are oriented to define the parameters of the investigation. We start from a general question and then those which are related with the epistemological objectives.

General research question

How do 750 listeners interact with the radio website?

Specific research questions

  • How do they consume the content?
  • What value does the radio provide?
  • Which are the website's pain points?

User hypotesis*

Radio lovers

People with an emotional connection with the radio. They start listening to it very early in the morning and have it all day on. They identify themselves ideologically with the radio.

Host/Shows fans

Users who have been fans of one of the hosts for years. They listen to their shows regardless of the radio they are on. These users are subdivided into those who identify ideologically and those who are fans of cultural programs in spite of the radio ideology.

Occasional users

They are people who occasionally listen to the radio. They may or may not be ideologically related.

*Based on data

Interviews

It was decided to carry out individual interviews since we needed to understand the individual experience and the emotional connection with the product, in addition to understanding the reasons for the actions that we saw reflected in the metrics. Individual interviews, allowed people to express more comfortably about their contexts, ideologies and frustrations. We conducted 16 interviews with people who were linked to the hypotesis that we had defined with the metrics.

Challenges

The COVID-19 pandemic forced us to conduct interviews remotely. When performing them during the first months of confinement, people were still learning how to use videoconference apps

More than 50% of our users are +55 years old and more than 20% are +65 years old. We estimated that there would be difficulties when trying to conduct interviews virtually

Insights

  • They value radio news briefings because occasional users are short on time.
  • Users do daily activities while listening to the radio, which sometimes causes them to lose information. This is why they value having a STOP button
  • Occasional users visit different online radio sites because it is the equivalent of “channel-hopping”.
  • They don't enter the site exclusively through the homepage, there are multiple entrances to the site. Many users search for the show or host they like on Google and go directly to the internal show page. They stay there to listen to the radio and navigate from that
  • Users do not scroll on the site, that is why they do not know that there is non-news content as in the case of radio dramas despite the fact that they express interest in listening to them.
  • For users it is very important to share content they listen on the radio especially on Facebook and Whatsapp, as conversation starters with their friends and family

UX Recommendations

Player

  • The current player does not provide the needs required by the user, so they look for it on other sites (Like Radiocut).
  • Currently the player is a button at the top of the site, this means that many casual users do not see it. A more modern player (Spotify Type) can be added at the bottom to make it more visible and allow more features to be added.
  • It is important not to remove the top button because users are used to using it.

New player

Information architecture

  • Users are used to going in and hit the player button. Currently the only thing they see when entering is a only featured new content.
  • It is necessary to show them the content in a different way so that they understand that there is more content available. It is suggested to add more entries in the first scroll to encourage navigation.
  • You can make a dynamic website that changes the content it displays depending on the time of day to match the radio air. Current news should appear in its morning version and starting at 7 pm, show on the front page notes related to cultural and humor shows.
  • Most of the users already know the programming or the schedule of their favorite program, therefore the space dedicated to this in the home page can be used in another way.

Proceso: Redesign architecture

Userflow

  • Users do not enter exclusively through the home as previously thought. Many google their favorite journalist and enter through the internal program to listen to the radio live.
  • The internal pages of the site are not designed to redirect the flow of the user to sections that are of interest to them.
  • It is suggested to redesign the "Show schedule" and "Shows" page so that they are not a dead end for the user and, instead, can capture areas of interest to increase the pages per session.

Process: Design of userflow to listen to an audio cut

Accessibily

  • Most of our users are over 40 years old. From that age, people begin to lose vision, hearing, and motor skills.
  • Several of our interviewees showed difficulties related to this type of inconvenience
  • It is suggested to adapt the website to the WCAG 2.1 (AA) standards in order to provide an accessible and inclusive site to all our users.
